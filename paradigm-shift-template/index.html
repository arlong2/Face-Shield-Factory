Provide free protective face shields to your community!

The COVID-19 Free Face Shield Project is a crowd-sourced model that you can replicate in your community to meet this critical shortage.

Start and Lead a Team

The role of the Team Leader is critical.

The power of this crowd-sourced factory model is having a centralized Team Leader coordinate a decentralized array of volunteer Makers, volunteers who do the 3D printing of the face shield’s frames safely in their own homes. No central printing space is required! And we have created a step-by-step guide to ensure your success.

Team Leaders are responsible for:

  • Managing the 3D printer Maker group and other volunteers to produce the frames and materials for the kits
  • Receiving batches of headset frames from Makers
  • Disinfecting the frames and packaging the face shield packages for customer orders
  • Accepting, recording, and fulfilling customer orders

The Team Leader should be:

  • An engineer or technically oriented business person
  • Organized, disciplined, and dedicated and willing to engage fulltime in management and coordination
  • Willing to learn easy-to-use Google tools such as Drive and spreadsheets, and Excel spreadsheets
  • Preferably owns a 3D printer, for rapid prototyping and Maker problem solving
  • In a home with no health care providers or children of K-12 age in house to minimize infection risk

Recruit Community Members to Help

The Face Shield Factory model depends on local volunteers.

You don’t have to be a Leader or own a 3D printer to make a difference. You can recruit people to fill these roles.

If you don’t know any candidates for leading a team, or even anyone who owns a 3D printer, you probably have a friend, or a friend of a friend, who does! Use your social networks and friends, and you will be surprised how soon you can get results!

Share this website with your friends and colleagues who you think would be interested.

In your communications, please pass on the following requirements so the right person can be found.

Requirements for a Team Maker:

  • Anyone who owns a 3D printer and is willing to print face shield headset frames in the safety and comfort of their own home

Start Printing Now!

A “Noble Army” of printer Makers is critical to the project’s success.

How to Start 3D Printing as a Maker

You can choose to print just the headset frames, or do that and also provide the other parts needed to assemble the face shields. The shield parts are bulk packaged and delivered in packages to the health care / critical worker recipients, who can quickly assemble the individual shields.

We strongly encourage you to register as a Maker so that you can be contacted by a Team Leader, who normally is responsible for providing the shield parts other than headset frames, so that you can concentrate on only printing frames and work efficiently as a team.

Complete instructions for how to start printing, along with printer .stl and .gcode files are avalible to be downloaded today!
