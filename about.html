Leading or Printing for a Crowdsourced Shield Factory



The need for PPE face shields to protect health care workers, first responders and critical service providers is urgent in most communities across the country. Traditional procurement is no longer reliable and shortages abound. Meanwhile, community members want to do something to help. But what to do and how to organize?

The COVID-19 Free Face Shield Factory project began with an ICU nurse neighbor’s need and a creative idea. By the next day, we built a shield with a 3D printed frame for her to take into her ICU. That afternoon, the head ICU MD asked for 70 ASAP! It was clear more capacity was needed to scale up to meet demand. By reaching out in our community to 3D printer owners, the crowd-source factory model was born. We are now organized as the “Noble Army” of 3D printer owners and support volunteers in large-scale production!

The business model for large scale production uses a centralized Team Leader who coordinates a decentralized team of 3D printer owners, who make the headset frames safely in their own homes. Other volunteers coordinate getting packaging supplies. The frames are periodically delivered to the Team Leader who disinfects and bulk-packages the frames along with plastic sheets, then delivers the packages with assembly instructions to end recipients, each of whom assembles his/her own shield easily in a minute or two.

Since April 1, 2020 we have produced thousands of face shields and are now delivering an average of over 200 each day to hospitals, first responders and critical care workers! And we offer these face shields free of charge to the people who need them.

We have developed a step-by-step guide with all the information, tools and instructions you need to get running quickly so you can meet the need for face shields in your community.

If you want to learn more about creating and leading a new face shield factory team in your area, click here: More About Starting a Team

If you are a 3D printer owner and want to join a factory team and/or start printing immediately on your own, click here: Start Printing!

Even if you don’t own a 3D printer and can’t lead a team, you can help by finding 3D printers and Team Leaders in your area by clicking here: Recruit People to Help!
